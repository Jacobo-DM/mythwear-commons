MythWear garments are body-centered: hoods, masks, bands, tabi, and leaf-circuits stitched into clothing — nothing that is not worn on the body.

Core Wearable Patterns

  • MythWear Hood · Arc–Spine Pattern
    A soft hood with spine-line channel and three anchor points at brow and temples, with a line over the crown and down the back.
    Includes optional “Beaded Sanctuary Module”: a quiet interior of wooden beads along the rim, crown, or spine-line — a portable cave for grounding, reflection, and classroom calm.
  • RootMask / RootFace · Nopal Lattice
    A ventilated face frame inspired by cactus rib structures — breath-first design, soft light, open sight.
  • Leaf Circuit · Wearable Nervous System
    A stitched or painted map of flow — a body-based nervous system for grounding, movement, and return.
  • Wrist–Elbow Band · Force Path Trainer
    Dual soft cuffs linked by a band that teaches the path of force through the forearm — a training companion for RageDance, writing posture, and mindful play.
  • Tabi + Ankle Band · Grounding Feet
    Split-toe tabi with a gentle Achilles band — “listening shoes” for quiet steps, balance, and grounding.

Modules

  • Beaded Sanctuary Module
    Portable cave interior for hoods and garments — grounding beads along rim, crown, or spine. Can be added to MythWear Hood, RootMask side panels, Leaf Circuit spine lines, and more.

Guides

  • Teacher’s Notes: Beaded Sanctuary Hood
    Classroom guide for trauma-aware, nonverbal regulation and calm. Explains how students can use the hood as a quiet signal: “I need a moment, but I am still here.”
